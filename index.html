Her Story

Mrs. Patience Odiba

Adishetu Patience Odiba was born on 20th September, 1972
to the family of Mallam Musa Amanyi and Abiba Musa Amanyi
both of Acharu-Egume in Dekina Local Government Area of Kogi State of Nigeria.
She lost some of her siblings to death as a result of Sickle cell anemia.
She attended LGEA Primary School,
Acharu-Egume between 1977 to 1983 where she obtained her First School Leaving Certificate,
after which she proceeded to Government Secondary School Egume between 1983 to 1988.
Adishetu Patience Odiba was admitted into College of Education Ankpa in Kogi State between 1991 to 1994
where she obtained her National Certificate in Education (NCE) on Economic Social Studies.

Despite the challenges of her ill health,
She refused to give up and went further to Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria Kano Campus in Federal College of Education (FCE) Kano,
between 1997 to 2000, where she obtained her Bachelor Degree (B.ed) in Social Studies.
She served as a Corp Member in Federal Ministry of Defence, FCT-Abuja between May 2001 to May 2002. Adishetu Patience Odiba experienced series of disappointment from her intended suitors as a single lady because of sickle cell anemia, many suitors deserted her. Adishetu Patience Odiba never knew herself to be sickler until 1997 when the result of her geno-type test before marriage came out to be (SS+F).She later went to 3 other standard hospital in Kano including Aminu Kano Teaching Hospital to confirm the geno-type test result, and all came out (SS+F) +F is Feotal hemoglobin Her God given husband whose genotype is AA and compatible with hers met her in her 300 level in the University in the year 1999 they both got married after her graduation in April 2001. They are blessed with children.

About SCDSF

Sickle cell disorder survivor foundation was incorporated with Nigeria Corporate Affairs Commission on the 25th September 2018. The foundation is a nongovernmental organization to serve humanity with the vision and mission to battle against these deadly genetic diseases (blood disorder) called sickle cell anemia which is claiming millions of lives on daily basis in the society.

AIMS AND OBJECTIVES

Creating awareness of what is sickle cell anemia, symptoms/ characteristic, dangers/ consequences of having sicklers as children in the family, possible remedies and way out.

Awareness campaign on the media of the appropriate geno-type best for intending couple, and on the one they must not dare to marry.

Creation of awareness on the available technologies that could help the patient of sickle cell anemia, e.g bone marrow transplant, and detection of SS gene on the carrier couple i.e (AS+AS) gene for avoidance of conception of sicklers.

To reduce to the barest minimum the numbers of married couple who are both carriers of the sickle cell gene

To reduce the number of sicklers born to a couple who are carriers of sickle cell gene.

Awaken the government to arise to the responsibility of managing and sustenance of people living with sickle cell condition.

Advocacy to government, NGO, Corporate organization, individuals, international organizations for the research into sickle cell disease.

Medical outreaches in collaboration with medical doctors and lab Scientist (Pathologist) to run genotype test for the suspected patient of sickle cell anemia in the communities and rural areas.

To advocate for the establishment of agencies, units or centres in states and local government area that will cater for sickle cell anemia patient

To promote a productive and self-sustaining youth, and woman capable of protecting themselves and their communities against sickle cell, malaria, HIV/ AIDS and violence.

MISSION AND VISION STATEMENT

To eradicate sickle cell anemia from the society through awareness campaign.

To carry out awareness and sensitization both in the rural and urban area about the grave consequences of sickle cell anemia.

To embark on medical outreaches in collaboration with medical Doctors and lab scientist (pathologist) to run genotype text for the suspected patient of sickle cell anemia in the communities and rural areas.

To ensure the intending couples are genotype compatible before marriage.

To advocate to the government for the establishment of agencies, units or centers in states and local government areas that will cater for sickle cell anemia patient.
